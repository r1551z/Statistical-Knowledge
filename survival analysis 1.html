Survival function, hazard function and density function

In this section we will have a brief introduction, about distribution related
functions for survival analysis.

Definition

In survival analysis, we view survival time T as a random variable, and we use
the following four functions to describe it.

cumulative distribution function: F(t)=P(T<t)F(t) = P(T < t)F(t)=P(T<t)
probability density function: f(t)=dF(t)dtf(t) = \frac{d{F(t)}}{dt}f(t)=dtdF(t)
survival function: S(t)=1F(t)S(t) = 1 - F(t)S(t)=1F(t)
hazard function: h(t)=dS(t)dtS(t)h(t) =-\frac{\frac{dS(t)}{dt}}{S(t)}h(t)=S(t)dtdS(t)
cumulative hazard function: H(t)=0th(x)dxH(t) =\int^t_0 h(x)dxH(t)=0th(x)dx

Relationship

h(t)=f(t)S(t)h(t) = \frac{f(t)}{S(t)}h(t)=S(t)f(t)

h(t)=dlog(S(t))dth(t) = -\frac{dlog(S(t))}{dt}h(t)=dtdlog(S(t))

S(t)=exp(H(t))S(t) = exp(-H(t))S(t)=exp(H(t))

Basic idea

Here the rir_iri, is known as the relative risk function, which is the hazard ratio between xix_ixi and the baseline.

partial likelihood

Cox PH model is a semi-parametric model, in the sense that the h0(t)h_0(t)h0(t) part (the baseline hazard function) in the hazard function is estimated in a non-parametric way. The parametric part of the likelihood function is estimated by solve the MLE of the following partial likelihood function:
jr(xj)iRjr(xi)\prod _j\frac{r(x_j)}{\sum_{i \in R_j}r(x_i)}jiRjr(xi)r(xj)

RjR_jRj is the cohort of subject at risk at time jjj, i.e, they are not censored or died yet.
